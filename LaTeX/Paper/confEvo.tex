\begin{abstract}
The endogenous nature of civil conflict has limited scholars' abilities to draw clear inferences about key drivers of conflict evolution. Using ACLED event data, we apply a novel network-based approach to trace the evolution of intrastate conflict dynamics. Through this network based approach we are able to better account for interdependent effects of different strategies: for example, we examine whether violence against civilians isolates groups from one another, or increases the probability of violence between them. We then use this information to predict conflict in Nigeria using an out-of-sample design. We compare these predictions to those generated using a model of intrastate conflict that assumes the dyadic interactions within this conflict network are independent.
\end{abstract}
